Synopsis

The Handmaid's Tale, acclaimed dystopian novel by Canadian author Margaret Atwood, published in 1985. The book, set in New England in the near future, posits a Christian fundamentalist theocratic regime in the former United States that arose as a response to a fertility crisis.
Offred’s recollections reveal that she was the daughter of a feminist activist. Before the advent of the theocratic government, the protagonist attended university and had a close friend named Moira. Offred became involved with a married man, eventually wed, and had a daughter. Following a military coup, the country became the Republic of Gilead. One day the protagonist was fired from her job at the library because women were no longer permitted to work. That evening she learned that women were also not allowed to have money, and her bank account had been transferred to Luke. They tried to flee to Canada with their daughter but were caught, and she was sent to a reeducation centre for indoctrination in preparation for becoming a Handmaid.
All women are assigned to various classes: the chaste childless Wives of the Commanders; the housekeeping Marthas; and the reproductive Handmaids, who turn their offspring over to the Wives and are called by the names of their assigned Commanders.

Characters

June Osborne The narrator and protagonist of The Handmaid’s Tale. Offred belongs to the class of Handmaids, fertile women forced to bear children for elite, barren couples. Handmaids show which Commander owns them by adopting their Commanders’ names, such as Fred, and preceding them with “Of.” Offred remembers her real name but never reveals it. She no longer has family or friends, though she has flashbacks to a time in which she had a daughter and a husband named Luke.
Serena Joy Waterford The Commander’s Wife, Serena worked in pre-Gilead days as a gospel singer, then as an anti-feminist activist and crusader for “traditional values.” In Gilead, she sits at the top of the female social ladder, yet she is desperately unhappy. Serena’s unhappiness shows that her restrictive, male-dominated society cannot bring happiness even to its most pampered and powerful women.
Fred Waterford The Commander is the head of the household where Offred works as a Handmaid. He was involved in designing and establishing Gilead. He initiates an unorthodox relationship with Offred, secretly playing Scrabble with her in his study at night. He often seems a decent, well-meaning man, and Offred sometimes finds that she likes him in spite of herself.
Aunt Lydia Clements The Aunts are the class of women assigned to indoctrinate the Handmaids with the beliefs of the new society and make them accept their fates. Aunt Lydia works at the “Red Center,” the re‑education center where Offred and other women go for instruction before becoming Handmaids. Although she appears only in Offred’s flashbacks, Aunt Lydia and her instructions haunt Offred in her daily life.
